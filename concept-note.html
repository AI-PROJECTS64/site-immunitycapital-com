Concept Note — ImmunityCapital.com

This Concept Note summarises the idea behind ImmunityCapital.com and the types of programmes it can host. It is written for Boards, executive committees, public health leaders, Chief Risk / Medical / Sustainability Officers and impact investors considering acquiring the domain.

1. What is “immunity capital”?

The expression immunity capital describes how immunity to infectious diseases can act as a form of capital in stratified societies. Historically, immunity (or the belief that someone was immune) has influenced access to jobs, credit, mobility and social status. Contemporary debates around COVID-19 and future pandemics show similar patterns, as vaccination status, prior infection or access to treatments can condition who is protected, who can work, travel or maintain income.

In this perspective, ImmunityCapital.com is a descriptive, neutral banner for work on:

  • mapping how immunity and protection are unequally distributed between and within countries;
  • linking immunity to labour markets, income, social protection and resilience;
  • designing policies, insurance solutions and investments that reduce protection gaps.

2. What ImmunityCapital.com is — and is not

2.1 What it can host

The domain can serve as a neutral label for:

  • Immunity Capital Observatory tracking immunity inequalities and protection gaps across countries, regions or groups;
  • Immunity Capital Index combining epidemiological, socio-economic and social protection indicators into a synthetic score;
  • Immunity Capital Lab — a research and policy programme on immunity, work, precarity, race, gender and migration;
  • Immunity Capital Impact Fund or similar vehicles dedicated to global health equity and inclusive insurance.

2.2 What it is not

ImmunityCapital.com is not:

  • a healthcare provider, hospital, clinic network or telemedicine platform;
  • a triage or scoring engine for individual decisions in HR, banking, insurance or migration;
  • a rating agency, label or certification on products, services or policies;
  • a regulated insurance, reinsurance, asset management or investment service.

The seller does not collect or process health data and does not provide medical, actuarial, legal, financial or tax advice. Any future use of the domain must be designed and operated under the acquirer’s own responsibility.

3. Why ImmunityCapital.com matters (2025–2035)

The COVID-19 crisis highlighted how unequal access to vaccines, diagnostics and treatments compounds pre-existing inequalities in mortality, morbidity and economic vulnerability. Similar issues will arise with future pandemics and emerging infectious diseases.

For public institutions, insurers, reinsurers, investors and philanthropies, immunity capital offers:

  • a way to name and analyse how health, work and income are linked;
  • a framework for measuring protection gaps and prioritising resources;
  • a narrative that connects pandemic preparedness, health equity and financial resilience.

An exact-match .com domain gives this concept a stable, easily communicable home that can outlive any single project, institution or technology stack.

4. Illustrative use cases

4.1 Immunity Capital Observatory

A global or regional observatory hosted under ImmunityCapital.com could publish dashboards on vaccination coverage, access to treatments and primary care, social protection and insurance coverage, highlighting where immunity capital is weakest and protection gaps are largest.

4.2 Immunity Capital Index

A composite index could summarise immunity capital at country, city or regional level, supporting analysis by international organisations, development banks, cities, NGOs and impact investors.

4.3 Immunity Capital Lab

A multi-stakeholder lab could bring together universities, think tanks, public institutions, insurers and cities to study the intersections of immunity with work, precarity, race, gender and migration, and to co-design policies and products that reduce protection gaps.

4.4 Immunity Capital Impact Fund

The domain could also front an impact fund investing in primary care, vaccine cold chains, digital health, inclusive insurance and other interventions that build immunity capital in under-served communities.

All use cases are illustrative only and do not describe existing projects. They do not bind the seller in any way.
